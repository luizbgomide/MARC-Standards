FIELD DEFINITION AND SCOPE

Unformatted note that describes the community information entity, e.g., a general description of a program and its various services, the mission statement/purpose of the organization. A listing of specific programs is contained in field 505 (Programs Note).

GUIDELINES FOR APPLYING CONTENT DESIGNATORS

■ INDICATORS

First Indicator - Display constant controller
Value that controls the generation of a display constant preceding the note.

■ SUBFIELD CODES

$a - Description note
Text of the note.

Examples of organizations:

520 ##$aToughlove is a nonprofit educational organization offering a program to help parents of adolescents and young people whose destructive behavior has gotten them in trouble at home, at school, in the community. A number of these children are substance abusers, so we offer parents information about drug and alcohol abuse and treatment. Toughlove groups are parent self-help groups giving and getting support in taking action to restore an atmosphere of cooperative living within the family. They do not act as professional counselors; instead they work with and refer members to other agencies in the community dealing with specific aspects of problem behavior. There are 12 groups meeting weekly in the Baltimore area. For group locations and contact phone numbers call (301) 252-9326.
520 ##$aThe society provides information about Down Syndrome and support of research and services for families and individuals. Call the hotline for information and referrals. Information pamphlets on Down Syndrome including "This Baby Needs You More." First copy free, $1 for each additional copy. Free 10-day loan of videocassette "Gifts of Love." (25 min.). $50 to purchase. Science Scholar Program: $25,000 per year for 2 yrs. to outstanding young post-doctoral researchers in Down Syndrome. Available through university sponsorship application. Annual Science Symposium on Down Syndrome by invitation.
520 ##$aThe purpose of this department is to strengthen the family by advocating for the family amid the complex of public and private organizations that carry the responsibility for its well-being. This office provides telephone service to assist individuals and families in accessing family support services. These resources may be either public or private organizations. The types of services available may include marriage and family counseling, drug and alcohol counseling, housing assistance, child care and job-training.
