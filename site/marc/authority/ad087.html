FIELD DEFINITION AND SCOPE

Single government document classification number, number stem, or a number span associated with a 1XX heading in an established heading record. The classification number(s) may be qualified by explanatory information when the heading is represented by more than one number in the same classification scheme.

Each classification number or number span associated with a heading is contained in a separate field 087. Government document classification number is an agency-assigned data element. It is assigned by designated agencies in government classification programs.

INPUT CONVENTIONS

Punctuation - Field 087 does not end with a period unless the field ends with an abbreviation, initial/letter, or other data that ends with a period.

Superintendent of Documents Classification numbers - One space is recorded between letters and numbers unless there is intervening punctuation.

Government of Canada Publications: Outline of Classification numbers - No spaces are recorded in the number.

Display Constants

- [hyphen associated with the content of subfield $b]
( ... ) [parentheses associated with the content of subfield $c]

Hyphen between the two class numbers in a number span in subfields $a and $b and the parentheses that may enclose the explanatory term in subfield $c are not carried in the MARC record. They may be system generated as display constants associated with the content of subfields $b and $c, respectively.

 
Content designated field:
087 ##$aC/G29/2$c1977-1987$2ordocs
Display example:
C/G29/2 (1977-1987)

Content of subfield $2 may be used to generate a display constant that identifies the source of the number.

CONTENT DESIGNATOR HISTORY

Field 087 - Dewey Classification Number Assigned in Canada [REDEFINED, 1997] [CAN/MARC only]
First indicator values were: # (No edition information recorded), 0 (Full edition), and 1 (Abridged edition). The second indicator values were: 0 (Assigned by NLC), 1 (Assigned by an agency other than NLC). The subfield codes were: $a (Dewey classification number-Single number or beginning number of a range), $b (Dewey classification number - end number of a range), $c (Explanatory term), ,$2 (Source (i.e. edition number)), and $5 (Library to which class number applies).
$0 - Authority record control number or standard number [NEW, 2017]
$1 - Real World Object URI [NEW, 2017]
