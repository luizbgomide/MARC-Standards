FIELD DEFINITION AND SCOPE

Name of a series when an event is part of a series.

GUIDELINES FOR APPLYING CONTENT DESIGNATORS

■ INDICATORS

First Indicator - Undefined
Undefined and contains a blank (#).
Second Indicator - Nonfiling characters
Value that specifies the number of character positions associated with a definite or indefinite article (e.g., The, An) at the beginning of a series title that are disregarded in sorting and filing processes.
0 - No nonfiling characters present
No initial article character positions are disregarded.Diacritical marks or special characters at the beginning of a series title field that does not begin with an initial article are not counted as nonfiling characters.
440 #0$aBaroque Concert Series
1-9 - Number of nonfiling characters present
A non-zero value indicates that a title begins with a definite or indefinite article that is disregarded in sorting and filing processes. Any diacritical mark, space or mark of punctuation associated with the article and any space or mark of punctuation preceding the first filing character after the article is included in the count of nonfiling characters. Any diacritic, however, associated with the first filing character is not included in the count of nonfiling characters.
440 #4$aThe Bible Studies Seminar

■ SUBFIELD CODES

$a - Title
Series title, exclusive of any name or number of a part/section.
440 #0$aLearning through Performers Program
$n - Number of part/section
Number designation for a part/section of a series title. Numbering is defined as an indication of sequencing in any form, e.g., Part 1, Section A.
440 #0$aDrug Rehabilitation.$nSeries IV
$p - Name of part/section
Name designation of a part/section of a series title.
440 #0$aSt. Louis Symphony Orchestra Lecture Series.$pChopin
$v - Sequential designation
Sequential designation associated with the series.
440 #0$a1993 Summer Season,$v35
$6 - Linkage
See the description of this subfield in Appendix A: Control Subfields.
$8 - Field link and sequence number
See the description of this subfield in Appendix A: Control Subfields.

INPUT CONVENTIONS

Punctuation - Field 440 does not end with a mark of punctuation unless the field ends with an abbreviation, an initial/letter, or other data that ends with a mark of punctuation.
